
# Core Concept

### 1. The Market Philosophy
This script does not subscribe to a specific market theory like Mean Reversion or Momentum. Instead, its "raison d’être" is to function as a **Quantitative Risk & Portfolio Management Framework**. It operates on the meta-level principle that any trading strategy, regardless of its underlying market philosophy, can only generate sustainable alpha if it is executed with rigorous, mathematically sound risk management. The script’s core belief is that a trader's primary edge comes not just from identifying profitable setups, but from systematically managing position sizing, expectancy, and drawdown. It seeks to exploit the mathematical law of large numbers by ensuring each trade is a statistically positive-expectancy bet within a defined risk structure, transforming trading from a series of discrete events into a cohesive business operation.

### 2. The Trade Narrative
The script does not look for a market setup; it assumes the trader has already found one. The "story" it requires begins *after* a trade signal has been identified. The narrative is one of pre-execution diligence: a trader has their entry, target, and stop-loss planned. Before committing capital, they turn to this calculator to answer critical questions: "Given my historical win rate and this trade's parameters, what is the mathematically correct position size? What is the trade's net expectancy after commissions? How does this single event fit into my long-term equity growth model and drawdown profile?" The script engages when a trader shifts focus from market analysis to risk application, creating a mandatory checkpoint between signal and execution.

### 3. Trigger Logic & Mechanics
The script's "trigger" is manual user input, which catalyzes a suite of interconnected calculations. It is not a market event but a deliberate act of planning.

*   **Why these indicators in tandem?** The confluence of `Winrate`, `Average R:R`, and `Risk %` is used to calculate **Positive Expectancy (EV)**, the foundational metric for determining if a strategy has a statistical edge. `Entry Price` and `Stop Loss %` are then used to translate this theoretical edge into a practical, risk-defined **Position Size**.
*   **How do filters reduce noise?** The script’s filters are not for price action but for decision-making. Calculations like **Ruin Probability**, **Kelly Criterion**, and **Stress Tests** (e.g., probability of 10 consecutive losses) serve to filter out emotionally-driven or mathematically unsound trades. They improve the signal-to-noise ratio of the trader's own execution by flagging when a proposed trade, despite a compelling chart pattern, carries an unacceptable risk to the portfolio. The catalyst from "observing" to "executing" is the synthesis of these inputs into a clear "Go/No-Go" dashboard on position viability and its portfolio impact.
