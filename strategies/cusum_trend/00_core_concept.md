
# Core Concept

### 1. The Market Philosophy

This strategy is rooted in **Momentum and Trend Persistence**, specifically designed to detect the inception of a new market regime. It operates on the principle that true trends do not emerge from random noise but from a persistent, cumulative shift in market pressure. The script's core philosophy is based on the CUSUM (Cumulative Sum) control chart, a statistical process control method adapted for financial markets. It aims to differentiate between random price fluctuations around a mean and a statistically significant deviation that signals a fundamental change in trend direction. The theoretical alpha is generated by entering early in a newly established trend, just after the signal has been statistically validated, and riding the subsequent momentum.

### 2. The Trade Narrative

The script patiently waits for a market that is either consolidating or exhibiting weak directional bias, where price oscillates around its Hull Moving Average (HMA) baseline. In this environment, the "pressure" accumulators remain dormant as price deviations are minor and classified as noise. The ideal setup unfolds as price begins to consistently close on one side of the HMA, with each successive bar adding evidence of a directional shift. This "pressure" builds incrementally, like a rising tide. The trade narrative is one of quiet accumulation followed by a decisive breach; the market must prove its directional intent over several periods before the script will acknowledge the new trend.

### 3. Trigger Logic & Mechanics

The trigger mechanism is a sophisticated confluence of a dynamic baseline and statistical validation.

*   **Why these indicators?** The **Hull Moving Average (HMA)** is used as a fast and smooth baseline to minimize lag. The core engine, however, is the **CUSUM algorithm**. Instead of a simple crossover, it calculates the `residual` (price deviation from the HMA) and accumulates it over time. This accumulation only occurs if the deviation exceeds a volatility-adjusted "drift" threshold (`k_drift`), effectively filtering out market noise.

*   **How do filters work?** The `h_thresh`, a multiple of the standard deviation of the residuals, acts as the ultimate signal filter. A trend is only confirmed when the accumulated pressure (`bullPressure` or `bearPressure`) surpasses this high-water mark. This ensures the signal is a result of persistent momentum, not a single anomalous price spike, thereby improving the signal-to-noise ratio.

*   **The Catalyst:** The script flips from "observing" to "executing" the moment the cumulative pressure breaches the `h_thresh` on a confirmed bar close. This event signifies that the evidence for a new trend is no longer anecdotal but statistically significant, triggering the declaration of a new bull or bear regime and its corresponding entry signal.
